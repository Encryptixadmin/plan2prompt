Proceed to STEP 4 — REQUIREMENTS INTELLIGENCE DEEPENING.

EXECUTION MODE:
Precision and depth.
No new UI flows.
No new modules.

OBJECTIVE:
Make generated requirements feel complete, deliberate, and hard to argue with.

--------------------------------
CORE PRINCIPLE
--------------------------------
Ambiguity upstream causes chaos downstream.
Requirements exist to remove ambiguity.

--------------------------------
REQUIREMENTS OUTPUT ENHANCEMENTS
--------------------------------

Enhance requirements generation to ALWAYS include the following explicit sections:

1. ASSUMPTIONS
- Clearly list assumptions the system depends on
- Include technical, user, and operational assumptions

2. OUT OF SCOPE
- Explicitly state what is NOT being built
- Prevent scope creep and misinterpretation

3. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
- For each major feature:
  - Clear conditions of success
  - Testable, observable outcomes

4. EDGE CASES & FAILURE MODES
- Known edge cases
- Expected failure behaviours
- What the system should do when things go wrong

5. NON-FUNCTIONAL CONSTRAINTS
- Performance expectations
- Scalability considerations
- Security boundaries
- Maintainability constraints

6. REQUIREMENT CONFIDENCE NOTES
- Identify areas with higher uncertainty
- Flag sections likely to change later

--------------------------------
QUALITY BAR
--------------------------------
The resulting requirements document should:
- Be suitable for handoff to a senior engineer
- Reduce follow-up questions
- Make downstream prompt generation safer

--------------------------------
DO NOT
--------------------------------
- Add diagrams or schemas yet
- Add industry or compliance templates
- Add AI explanation prose
- Change locking or versioning behaviour

--------------------------------
REVIEW CHECKPOINT
--------------------------------
After implementation:
- Show an example before/after requirements output
- Explain how ambiguity is reduced
- Confirm no sequencing or UX rules changed

WAIT for confirmation before proceeding.
